Neon Screws & Bolts

Neon Screws & Bolts is an addictive, high-fidelity 2D gravity physics puzzle game set in a cyberspace mainframe. Unlatch yellow bolts to release one end of a hanging neon pink rod, watch it swing realistically under gravity as a pendulum (carrying the unscrewed bolt along on its tip), and lock it into a new wall hole to navigate across obstacles. Avoid letting both screws loose, plan around cyan firewalls, align with shifting sine-wave core holes, and land both ends safely in target green goal zones to decrypt the core mainframe!

🕹️ How to Play

Maneuver the neon pink rod safely from the starting position to the green glowing goal holes.

  • Select Bolt: Tap/Click any occupied yellow bolt to unscrew it.
    • *Locked Screw Protection:* If only one bolt is holding the rod, it is locked 🔒 and glows red to prevent accidental drops! You must screw the free bolt in first.
  • Carried Swings: When unscrewed, the bolt travels WITH the swinging tip of the rod. The rod rotates smoothly under pendulum gravity around the other locked screw.
  • Maneuver and Snap: Time your taps perfectly! Tap any empty cyan hole within a 40px radius of the swinging bolt to insert and lock it, changing your pivot point.
  • Avoid Crashes: Do not let the rod hit obstacles, and NEVER release the last support screw—letting the rod fall triggers a FAIL state instantly!

🏆 Hacker Pro Strategies

  • Use momentum! High swings build swing height, allowing you to reach distant wall holes.
  • Bouncing off cyan firewalls is extremely useful to alter your swing arc direction.
  • In gravity shift zones, anticipate that downward is now upward—adjust your release timings accordingly!
  • Use par moves wisely to secure 3-star terminal decrypt ratings.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Star Rating system?

Levels award 1 to 3 gold stars based on the number of moves used versus the level's benchmark par limit. Move efficiently to secure a perfect 3-star rating!

Why does the screw glow red?

If only one screw is pinning the rod, it is locked (🔒) to prevent the rod from falling to its death. You must insert the active screw into a hole before removing this last support.
